Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest in the solar system.
Saturn has 146 moons, making it the planet with the most moons in the solar system.
Jupiter has 95 moons.
The largest moon of Saturn is Titan, which is larger than the planet Mercury.
Saturn is known for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock.

Here are five key facts about Titan:
Substantial Atmosphere: It is the only moon in the solar system with a dense, thick atmosphere, composed primarily of nitrogen and methane.
Liquid Lakes and Rivers: Titan has a surface with lakes, rivers, and seas of liquid methane and ethane, creating a, similar to Earth’s water cycle, but at extremely cold temperatures.
Larger than Mercury: With a diameter of 3,200 miles (5,150 km), it is the second-largest moon in the solar system (after Jupiter's Ganymede) and is actually bigger than the planet Mercury.
